The Prats - from all cultures - uses the same calendar which is the Life Calendar. Here's how it works:

  • Every day has 24 hours (conventionally exact), split to 4 sections:
    1. From 00 to 06 hour: Pre-Mateno
    2. From 06 to 12 hour: Mateno
    3. From 12 to 18 hour: Post-Mateno
    4. From 18 to 24 hour: Nokto
  • This is also why a clock on Pratero is either digital or 6 hours analog where it would cycle 4 times a day
  • A week on Pratero only has 4 days: 3 weekdays and 1 weekend
  • A month has 8 weeks, or 8*4 = 32 days
  • A year has 12 months (unsuprisingly), so it would have 384 days

But the actual reason why it is called the Life Calendar is because its first year (Year 0) as the estimated year when life started existing on the planet

The calendar was developed in the middle age in Homa, but takes a long time to decide Year 0. By the time it was decided and thus bring the calendar to use, the year is 400 137 652
